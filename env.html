Visualizing Unseen Environmental Impacts: Environmental Racism

Course: Visual Communications 1 - Spring 2023 - 17” x 22” Poster

This graphic poster created in Adobe Photoshop shows air pollution disproportionately affecting communities of color. The composition visually illustrates this inequality by dividing clear and clean air from polluted air with a thick red line representative of redlining. Redlining has left communities of color disproportionately in low-income hazardous environments that affect their health; the red line down the center of the composition alludes to that discriminatory practice. The hands are used to show that black people, black children, make up a smaller fraction of the U.S. population in comparison to white people and yet are affected by climate change more rapidly and at higher percentages. The color palette and position of the hands are representative of the American flag as a way to convey that this inequality is systemic. The contrast of color and scale is used to highlight what viewers should prioritize to get them thinking about the intersectionality of climate change, racism, and the United States history of antiblackness.
